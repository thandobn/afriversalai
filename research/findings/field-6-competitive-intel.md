## Provider Deep Dives

### College Africa Group (CAG)
- **What they sell:** One-day tool tutorial courses — ChatGPT for Business, Microsoft Copilot, AI for HR, AI + Excel, AI for Finance teams. Also "AI in the Workplace" as a multi-week corporate program.
- **Delivery:** Onsite, virtual, hybrid — nationwide reach across all SA provinces. This is their main competitive advantage: distribution.
- **Accreditation:** SETA-accredited for some programs (Excel, Project Management) but **NOT for their AI courses**. This is a gap they have not closed.
- **Content depth:** Purely tool-tutorial. Zero critical evaluation component. No assessment of whether learners can judge AI outputs — only whether they can use tools.
- **African context:** None. Content is generic Western corporate.
- **Pricing:** Opaque. Volume discounts mentioned for corporate teams. Likely R3,000–R8,000/person/day for onsite corporate.
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM. They have reach and existing corporate relationships in HR/finance. Their content is shallow; their brand is established.

### Prospen Africa
- **What they sell:** Role-based AI training at executive/manager level — AI for Leaders & Managers (5 days), Mini-MBA: AI & Digital Transformation (5 days), AI for HR (3-5 days), AI for Finance & Accounting (3-5 days), AI for Procurement (3-5 days), AI for Office Managers (3-5 days), AI for Executive Assistants.
- **Delivery:** In-person at Sandton and Durban venues; some online availability.
- **Accreditation:** Not listed. No SETA accreditation found.
- **Content depth:** More strategic than CAG — covers "AI in the African business environment," governance, and decision-making. Mentions "African-focused case studies" but does not document them.
- **African context:** Claimed but unsubstantiated. Language of "African business environment" appears in marketing without proof points.
- **Pricing:** Opaque. 5-day executive programs likely R8,000–R20,000+/person given venue and duration.
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM-HIGH. They have already moved into role-based track model AND African context framing — Funda's two positioning pillars. However, their content is for executives (not middle professionals) and their "African context" is marketing language, not operational proof.

### AAAI (African Academy of AI)
- **What they sell:** AI Accelerator (5-track program from awareness to productivity), 72-Hour AI Masterplan (3-day strategic intensive with 90-day execution plan), AI governance frameworks, executive coaching.
- **Partners:** GIBS, Henley, UCT, African Association of Business Schools — credentialed through these institutions.
- **Delivery:** Online, in-company sessions, on-demand.
- **Accreditation:** Partner institution credentials (Henley, UCT) — not SETA accreditation.
- **Content depth:** Most sophisticated of the three for executive/governance layer. 5-track structure from "basic awareness to measurable productivity" is their differentiator.
- **African context:** Present in marketing, partner network includes African business schools. More credibly grounded than Prospen.
- **Pricing:** Opaque. Business school partnerships suggest premium pricing (R15,000–R40,000+ for programs).
- **Threat level:** HIGH for executive segment. LOW for Funda's middle-professional target — AAAI is not competing for the same learner.

### NobleProg
- **What they sell:** Technical AI training — NLP, ML, computer vision, Azure AI, AWS AI, ChatGPT, AI agents, Edge AI. 21 subcategories. 4–35 hours per course.
- **Delivery:** Online live training and onsite. Available in JHB, Cape Town, Durban, Pretoria.
- **Accreditation:** Not found.
- **Content depth:** Technical depth for developers/engineers. Not relevant for business professional AI literacy.
- **Threat level:** LOW. Different audience entirely.

### UCT / Henley
- **UCT:** "AI, Ethics & Policy in Africa" — 5-week online course, 2 sessions/week. Most academic of all — includes ethics and policy angle. Price not found but likely R8,000–R20,000.
- **Henley:** "Strategies for Advanced AI Leadership in Africa" — executive leadership positioning.
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM for credentialing comparison. Learners may compare Funda's certificate to UCT. However, UCT/Henley are expensive, slow to enroll, and not designed for corporate team cohorts.

## ICASA Proximity Assessment

No SA competitor appears to have an explicit ICASA relationship or engagement with regulatory-sector staff. AAAI has business school partnerships (GIBS, UCT); CAG has distribution reach; Prospen has executive networks. None have a foothold in the regulatory/government sector via ICASA.

**Funda's ICASA connection is a genuine first-mover advantage in the government/regulatory sector.**

## Pricing Intelligence

All SA B2B AI training providers use opaque pricing (quote-on-request). This is standard in SA corporate L&D. What can be inferred:
- 1-day corporate courses (CAG model): likely R3,000–R8,000/person
- 3-5 day executive programs (Prospen model): likely R8,000–R20,000+/person
- University-affiliated programs (UCT/Henley): likely R15,000–R40,000+/person
- MICT SETA learnership stipends: R2,500–R6,500/month (for formal learnerships, different product)

Funda's hypothesis of R6,000–R12,000/person for the core track is in the middle of the market — above CAG's day courses, below university programs. With SETA accreditation, post-grant cost drops to R2,400–R7,200/person, making Funda significantly more price-competitive than any currently SETA-accredited alternative (because none exist for AI).

## Strategic Takeaways for Funda

1. **Move fast on SETA:** No competitor has SETA accreditation for AI. First to accredit creates a structural advantage — corporate buyers can recover 40-60% of costs. Begin inquiry in Year 1 after pilot validates curriculum.
2. **The middle professional is unclaimed:** CAG targets admin/HR/finance staff with shallow content. AAAI and Prospen target executives. Nobody is doing well-designed, assessed AI literacy for the professional layer between these two — the communications officers, operations managers, analysts, and team leads.
3. **Prospen is the closest competitor on positioning** — they already claim "African context" and role-based tracks. Funda's differentiation must be *operational*: documented case studies with named SA examples, assessment rubrics that test judgment (not just completion), and a verifiable competency credential.
4. **Pricing should stay opaque in the short term** — consistent with market norms. Quote-on-request is standard. The pricing hypothesis (R6,000–R12,000) is a starting point for buyer conversations, not a public price.
5. **Track naming matters:** Prospen already owns "AI for [Function]" naming (AI for HR, AI for Finance, etc.). Funda should consider differentiated naming that signals the judgment angle — e.g., "AI Judgment for Finance Professionals" or "Thinking with AI: Finance Edition."
