## 1. The Structural Demand Driver: B-BBEE Skills Development

This is the most important thing to understand about selling corporate training in South Africa. The demand is not discretionary — it is legally mandated.

### How It Works

South African companies above certain revenue thresholds are rated against the B-BBEE (Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment) scorecard. **Skills Development is a priority element worth up to 25 points** (20 base + 5 bonus) on that scorecard.

**The payroll spend targets:**
- **Generic Scorecard** (companies with annual turnover >R50M): must spend **6% of annual payroll** on qualifying training to earn maximum Skills Development points
- **QSE Scorecard** (companies with turnover R10M–R50M): must spend **3% of annual payroll**

**The penalty for underperforming:** Because Skills Development is a "priority element," failing to achieve 40% of the target triggers an automatic **one B-BBEE level downgrade**. A level downgrade affects preferential procurement scores — meaning clients and government may stop buying from a company that drops a level. This is an existential business risk, not an abstract compliance concern.

**Practical implication for Funda:** An SA company with a R100M payroll is mandated to spend R6M per year on qualifying training. A Funda cohort for 20 people at R10,000/person = R200,000. That is 3.3% of their annual Skills Development budget. This is not a hard sell — it is a budget that must be spent.

### The Skills Development Levy (SDL) — The Money Flow

Companies with a payroll >R500,000 per year pay a **1% Skills Development Levy (SDL)** to SARS (SA Revenue Service) on top of their other taxes. That money flows to the relevant SETA for the sector.

Companies that:
1. Submit a **Workplace Skills Plan (WSP)** to their SETA at the start of the year
2. Submit an **Annual Training Report (ATR)** at year end
3. Use **SETA-accredited training providers**

...can claim back **40–60% of their SDL as discretionary grants.**

**The double benefit of SETA accreditation:**
- SETA-accredited training counts toward B-BBEE Skills Development points AND enables SDL recovery
- Non-SETA training can still count for B-BBEE points, but the company cannot recover its SDL
- Net effect: a company using Funda (SETA-accredited) at R10,000/person may recover R4,000–R6,000/person. Effective cost: R4,000–R6,000/person. A company using a non-accredited competitor at R8,000/person pays R8,000/person.

**This is the single most important commercial fact for Funda's go-to-market:** SETA accreditation is not a bureaucratic nice-to-have. It is a pricing mechanism that makes Funda the cheapest option in the market for corporate buyers who are already mandated to spend on training.

## 2. MICT SETA Accreditation — The Path

Funda's target sector (ICT/telecoms) falls under the **MICT SETA** (Media, Information and Communication Technology Sector Education and Training Authority). The accreditation process for a new training provider:

### Process Steps

| Step | Action | Who |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Submit Letter of Intent for Accreditation | Funda → QCTO |
| 2 | QCTO assesses and issues referral letter | QCTO → Funda |
| 3 | Submit Proof of Evidence (PoE) package to MICT SETA | Funda → MICT SETA |
| 4 | MICT SETA evaluates PoE | MICT SETA |
| 5 | Accreditation granted (or feedback for gaps) | MICT SETA → Funda |

### Requirements for the PoE Package (key items)

- Programme/curriculum aligned to NQF (National Qualifications Framework) unit standards
- Suitably qualified facilitators (relevant qualification + facilitation credential)
- Registered assessors and moderators (SETA-registered)
- Quality Management System documentation
- Evidence of capacity to deliver (venues, materials, learner management)

### Timeline

Currently **3–4 months** from Letter of Intent to accreditation (the process was recently streamlined through QCTO centralization). This is significantly faster than the previous 6–18 month timelines.

### Strategic Timing Recommendation

Begin the accreditation inquiry (not application — just understanding requirements) in Year 1 alongside the pilot, per the Section 10 action plan. The pilot will produce the curriculum evidence needed for the PoE. The curriculum exists before the accreditation; the accreditation validates the curriculum.

**Do not attempt to accredit before the pilot.** The PoE requires evidence of delivery quality, and the pilot is the first evidence.

## 3. The ICASA Licensee Map — Funda's Pipeline

ICASA licenses all companies operating electronic communications networks and services in South Africa. The connection to someone at ICASA is a connection to the regulator of the following:

### Major Licensees (Direct Training Prospects)

| Company | Type | Size | B-BBEE obligation | Training budget |
|---------|------|------|------------------|----------------|
| **Vodacom** | Mobile + broadband | 20,000+ employees | Generic scorecard (6% payroll) | Large — has own academy |
| **MTN South Africa** | Mobile | 15,000+ employees | Generic scorecard | Large — has own academy |
| **Telkom** | Fixed-line + mobile + IT | 20,000+ employees | Generic scorecard | Large |
| **Cell C** | Mobile | ~1,500 employees | Generic scorecard | Medium |
| **MultiChoice** | Broadcasting | ~4,000 employees | Generic scorecard | Medium-Large |
| **Rain** | Mobile data | ~500 employees | QSE/Generic | Medium |
| **SABC** | Public broadcasting | ~3,500 employees | Generic (state entity) | Medium |
| **Liquid Intelligent Technologies** | Fibre + cloud + data | ~1,000 employees | QSE/Generic | Medium |
| **BCX (Telkom subsidiary)** | ICT services | ~6,500 employees | Generic scorecard | Large |
| **Dimension Data (NTT)** | ICT services | ~5,000 in SA | Generic scorecard | Large |
| **400+ smaller licensees** | Various ICT | Variable | QSE or Generic | Variable |

### The Strategic Value of the ICASA Connection

An ICASA contact does not give Funda a client. It gives Funda:
1. **Introductions** to L&D contacts at ICASA's licensees — warm referrals carry far more weight than cold outreach
2. **Regulatory context** — understanding of what skills gaps ICASA is observing across the ICT sector (ICASA publishes annual State of ICT reports and interacts with licensees on compliance)
3. **Potential pilot** — ICASA itself as a government organization has training obligations and would be a prestige first client (note: government procurement timelines apply — 3-9 months)
4. **Market signal** — "Funda engaged with ICASA" is a credibility statement to any ICT company that knows the regulatory landscape

### Pilot Organization Targeting Criteria

Based on Field 0 (sector prioritization) findings, the ideal first pilot organization is:
- A mid-size ICT sector company (not MTN/Vodacom — too large, procurement too slow for first pilot)
- 50–500 employees (L&D manager with discretionary budget authority; no 6-month procurement cycle)
- Has a named MICT SETA levy-paying relationship (eligible for SDL recovery)
- Has a B-BBEE compliance requirement driving training spend
- Willing to be a design partner, not just a training customer (provide feedback, iterate, co-design case studies)

**Candidates to investigate via ICASA connection:** mid-size ISPs, regional telecoms, ICT consultancies, and smaller licensees where procurement decisions are made quickly.

## 4. Competitive Context: Microsoft SA AI Initiative

**This is the largest single force in the SA AI training market and Funda must understand it clearly.**

Microsoft SA committed in early 2025 to train 1 million South Africans in AI skills by 2026. As of 2026: 4 million engaged, 1.4 million trained, nearly 500,000 credentialed.

**What Microsoft is doing:** Copilot training, Azure AI tools, cybersecurity, digital skills — Microsoft product certifications. Free or heavily subsidized. Delivered via TVET colleges, online, and SABC.

**Why this matters for Funda:**

| Dimension | Microsoft SA Initiative | Funda |
|-----------|------------------------|-------|
| Scale | Millions of learners | Tens to hundreds (initially) |
| Content | Microsoft product tool use | AI judgment, critical evaluation |
| Target | All South Africans | Corporate + government professionals |
| Credential | Microsoft certification | Funda certificate (SETA goal) |
| Price | Free / subsidized | R6,000–R12,000/person (SDL recoverable) |
| Depth | Tool familiarity | Genuine literacy + agency |

**The opportunity in Microsoft's wake:** Microsoft's initiative creates AI awareness at scale in SA. It trains people to use tools. When those same people — now aware of AI, now using Copilot — realize they don't understand what the tool is doing or when not to trust it, they are ready for Funda.

**Positioning language:** "Microsoft taught your team to use the tools. Funda teaches them to think with the tools."

**The headwind:** Microsoft's free certification creates a "why pay for training when it's free?" comparison point. The answer: Microsoft certifications don't test judgment, don't carry SETA credentials, and don't come with a facilitator who understands the African professional context.

## 6. SA L&D Buyer Language — How They Talk About Training

This is critical for Funda's pitch and marketing materials. SA corporate L&D buyers speak in B-BBEE compliance and SDL mechanics terminology. Using their language removes procurement friction.

**Words and phrases that matter to an SA HR/L&D buyer:**

| Their language | What it means | Funda's relevance |
|----------------|--------------|-------------------|
| "SETA accredited" | Training that qualifies for SDL recovery | Funda's Year 1 target |
| "NQF aligned" / "NQF Level X" | Registered on the National Qualifications Framework | Required for SETA |
| "WSP eligible" | Can be included in Workplace Skills Plan submission | Yes (with SETA accreditation) |
| "SDL claimable" / "SDL recoverable" | Can recover levy costs via discretionary grant | Yes (with SETA accreditation) |
| "Skills Development spend" | Their B-BBEE scorecard training budget line | Funda fits here |
| "Pivotal program" | Training that addresses identified scarce/critical skills | AI is pivotal by definition |
| "Learnership" | Formal SETA structured learning pathway with stipend | Different product from Funda's cohort |
| "POPIA compliant training" | Training processes handle learner data per SA law | Must be built in |

**What an SA L&D director is actually optimizing for:**
1. Can I claim this against my SDL and recover costs?
2. Does this count toward my B-BBEE Skills Development element and keep me from dropping a level?
3. Will employees actually complete it? (Completion is their KPI, not just enrollment)
4. Can I justify this to my CFO as a B-BBEE compliance investment?

**Funda's pitch to an L&D director (in their language):**
*"Funda is building toward MICT SETA accreditation. Our AI literacy cohort for your staff qualifies for your B-BBEE Skills Development scorecard, and once accredited, will enable discretionary grant recovery through MICT SETA. We deliver in 6 cohort sessions with a certified facilitator — completions, not enrollments."*

## 7. Founder Credibility — Establishing Without Prior Credential

Isabella Morales (council review) flagged this as critical: how does Thando establish credibility as the person who walks into an ICASA conversation?

**Research synthesis: African B2B trust is relationship-built, not credential-displayed.**

What the research shows about establishing credibility in African institutional B2B markets:
1. **Personal relationships outperform formal credentials.** The ICASA connection IS the credibility entry. A warm referral from a known contact carries more weight than a CV.
2. **Content-based authority.** Publishing research establishes expertise. The needs-analysis.html document Funda has already produced is itself a credibility artifact — it shows deep SA-specific research that most competitors have not done.
3. **Value demonstration before close.** African institutional buyers want to see thinking, not just titles. Sharing the research document with a pilot prospect (not as marketing, as a conversation starter) shows the depth of preparation.
4. **Pilot as the permanent credential.** The first paid pilot produces a case study. That case study is the credential. Everything before it is the setup.

**Thando's specific credibility assets:**
- Design Engineer (Caltech ME): demonstrates rigorous analytical thinking — different from typical training background, but applicable to course design precision
- Research depth: the needs-analysis document is evidence of a level of market understanding that most SA training providers don't have
- ICASA connection: warm institutional introduction to the regulatory sector
- B-BBEE and SETA knowledge: most training providers do not understand the buyer's compliance context as well as Funda already does
- African diaspora perspective: not a Western outsider parachuting in; genuine connection to Zimbabwe context through family; understanding of African professional reality

**What Thando should not lead with:** title, credential, or time in the AI field. Lead with the research, the mission, and the understanding of the buyer's B-BBEE compliance challenge.

**First conversation framework (ICASA contact → ICT company introduction):**
> "I'm building an AI literacy training program specifically for South African professionals — not tool tutorials, but the judgment skills your teams need when AI is making or influencing decisions. I've spent the last [X months] researching the SA market and I think there's a gap that nobody is filling well. I'd love your perspective before I finalize the curriculum — would you be willing to have a 30-minute conversation?"

This is a research conversation, not a sales conversation. It builds relationship, gathers buyer intelligence (Field 1 primary research), and positions Thando as someone worth listening to.

## 9. Professional Body & CPD Channel — Parallel Sales Pathway

Professional associations are an underused distribution channel that Funda can access before SETA accreditation. Every accredited profession in SA requires members to complete annual Continuing Professional Development (CPD) hours — meaning there is built-in demand for learning that counts toward compliance. One CPD partnership with a professional body gives Funda access to their full membership without cold outreach.

### Key Bodies to Target (by sector alignment)

| Professional Body | Members | CPD requirement | Funda relevance |
|------------------|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **SAICA** (SA Institute of Chartered Accountants) | 40,000+ | 40 CPD hours/year | Finance track anchor; AI in financial analysis, POPIA compliance |
| **SABPP** (SA Board for People Practices) | HR profession | CPD-mandatory | AI in recruitment, performance review, L&D — direct overlap with competency 6, 13 |
| **Law Society of SA** | Attorneys | CPD points required | Employment Equity AI liability (Case 007), POPIA, automated decisions |
| **SA Institute of Internal Auditors** | Risk/audit practitioners | CPD-mandatory | AI governance, accountability chains, audit of AI systems |
| **Association for Project Management SA** | Project managers | CPD points | AI use in project planning and risk management |
| **HPCSA** (Health Professions Council) | Healthcare professionals | CPD-mandatory | AI in clinical decision support, Case 009 |

### Channel Strategy

1. Approach the CPD committee of one professional body with a one-page proposal
2. Offer a 90-minute AI literacy workshop as a CPD event for their members (low barrier — they organize it, Funda delivers it)
3. Use the workshop as the lead-gen pipeline into the 6-week Core Track
4. Apply for formal CPD recognition of the Core Track (usually requires application and curriculum review)

**Why SAICA first:** SAICA has 40,000+ members, strong institutional credibility, and clear AI use cases in financial services. A SAICA-endorsed workshop positions Funda as the AI literacy standard for the SA accounting profession. That credibility transfers to every subsequent finance-sector conversation.

**Critical point on timing:** This channel can be pursued before SETA accreditation because CPD recognition is a different (lighter) process. It does not require NQF registration. It requires content review and proof of facilitator competence. Funda's research document IS the content evidence.

## 10. Procurement-Ready Assets — What to Produce Before the First Sales Conversation

The ICASA conversation is a research conversation, not a sales conversation (per Section 7). But the moment a buyer asks "can you send me more information?" these need to exist. Produce them in priority order.

| Asset | What it contains | Priority |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| **One-page capability statement** | Who Funda is, what problem it solves, why it's different — written for an L&D or procurement contact | 🔴 Before first meeting |
| **Course brochure (1-2 pages)** | Core Track structure, learning outcomes, schedule, assessment approach, certificate | 🔴 Before first meeting |
| **Facilitator bio (Thando)** | Background, design engineering rigor, SA market research, what qualifies her to lead this — emphasize depth of research, not traditional training credential | 🔴 Before first meeting |
| **Pricing sheet** | Core Track rates, Manager Add-on, 90-minute Workshop entry price — presented as investment ranges, not fixed prices | 🟡 Before any pricing conversation |
| **Sample session agenda** | What 2 hours of a Funda session looks like — Encounter, Reflect, Concept, Apply with time allocations | 🟡 Before buyer asks |
| **Certificate sample** | Visual mock of the certificate; the exact wording: "demonstrated AI judgment competency" | 🟡 Before buyer asks |
| **Assessment rubric (summary)** | How learners are evaluated; what "demonstrated competency" means in practice; 1-page version of the Field 5 rubric | 🟡 Before buyer asks |
| **Data/privacy statement** | How learner data is handled during the course (POPIA-compliant) | 🟡 Before any contract discussion |
| **Pilot offer letter template** | Standard terms for a design-partner pilot — discount in exchange for feedback, testimonial, case study permission | 🟠 Before pilot negotiation |
| **B-BBEE status / entity documentation** | Funda's B-BBEE level (if operating as a SA entity); tax clearance; vendor registration | 🟠 Required for formal procurement |

**Practical sequence:** Capability statement → Brochure → Bio → Pricing sheet. Those four get Funda through 90% of early sales conversations. The rest come when the buyer starts asking process questions.
