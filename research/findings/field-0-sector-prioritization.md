## The Central Question

**"Which ONE sector will write the first check fastest — and therefore which track do we build first alongside the Core Track?"**

This is not "which sector needs AI literacy most." That question produces a different answer (public education, public healthcare) and does not produce revenue. This question is about market entry speed.

---

## Sectors Evaluated

### 1. SA ICT / Telecommunications Sector

**Companies:** MTN, Vodacom, Telkom, BCX (Telkom subsidiary), Dimension Data (NTT), MultiChoice, Rain, Cell C, Liquid Intelligent Technologies, and dozens of mid-size ICT companies.

**AI urgency:** Very high. Digital transformation is their core business. AI is being deployed into operations, customer service, network management. Staff AI literacy is a recognized gap.

**Training budget:** Significant and mandated. ICT companies fall under the **MICT SETA** (Media, Information and Communication Technology Sector Education and Training Authority). B-BBEE Skills Development obligations are enforced. MTN and Vodacom run their own academies but focus on technical skills, not professional AI literacy.

**Procurement speed (private sector):** 4–10 weeks for L&D purchasing decisions at mid-size contract level (not a tender process). L&D managers typically have discretionary budget authority up to a threshold (commonly R100,000–R500,000) before needing executive approval.

**ICASA connection leverage:** This is the critical factor. ICASA *licenses and regulates* every major SA ICT company. An ICASA contact gives Funda:
- Warm introductions to licensing-compliance staff at MTN, Vodacom, Telkom
- Credibility as a program that has engaged with the regulatory environment
- Potential for an ICASA staff cohort as a pilot (government entry point)
- Signal to ICT licensees that Funda has government-adjacent credibility

**Existing AI training programs:** Technical (developer) tracks exist internally. Business professional AI literacy is sparse — the gap that Funda fills.

**Verdict: STRONGEST first-target sector.** Fast procurement, mandatory training spend, ICASA connection is the clearest leverage point, and the middle-professional gap is real.

---

### 2. SA Finance & Banking Sector

**Companies:** Standard Bank, FNB (First National Bank), Nedbank, Absa, Capitec, Discovery, Sanlam, Old Mutual, Momentum, African Bank.

**AI urgency:** Extremely high and documented. The FSCA/PA published SA's first comprehensive AI-in-finance report (November 2025). Key finding: **insufficient talent and skills is the single biggest constraint to AI scaling** across banking, insurance, payments, and lending. 50%+ of banks plan R20M+ AI investment.

**Training need specifics from FSCA/PA report:**
- Staff need AI understanding (not just technical AI — business professional literacy)
- Model risk management and governance
- AI transparency and explainability
- POPIA compliance in AI contexts
- This is exactly Funda's curriculum scope — judgment, not just tools

**Training budget:** Large. Financial sector has significant L&D spend and active B-BBEE compliance through **INSETA** (Insurance SETA) and **FASSET** (Finance Sector SETA).

**Procurement speed (private sector):** Similar to ICT — 6–12 weeks for L&D decisions. Financial sector L&D teams are sophisticated buyers.

**Existing AI training programs:** Largely for technical/data science staff. Business professional AI literacy at the operations, compliance, and customer-facing level is under-served.

**Verdict: STRONG second sector.** The FSCA/PA report is a powerful door-opener — Funda can reference it directly in the sales pitch. No ICASA connection advantage here, but no ICASA needed either.

---

### 3. SA Government Sector (ICASA, DCDT, National Treasury, provincial departments)

**AI urgency:** High at the policy level. DCDT's National AI Policy Framework (approved Cabinet March 2026) identifies skills capacity as Pillar 1. Genuine mandate.

**Training budget:** Exists but subject to Public Finance Management Act (PFMA). Training spend is line-itemed in departmental budgets aligned to SA's April financial year.

**Procurement speed:** SLOW. Formal tender process (RFQ or open tender depending on value). Minimum 3 quotes. Supply Chain Management approval. For contracts above thresholds: advertised tender with 21+ day notice period. Total timeline: **3–9 months minimum** from initial conversation to signed contract. Budget must be allocated in the prior year's departmental plan.

**ICASA specifically:** As a portfolio organization of DCDT, ICASA follows government procurement rules. An ICASA pilot is possible but requires budget pre-allocation and formal procurement process. The ICASA connection is most valuable as a credibility anchor and introduction network, not as a fast first client.

**Verdict: HIGH VALUE for credibility, SLOW for revenue. Strategy: Pursue ICASA relationship to open ICT sector doors; use ICASA pilot as a parallel slow-burn (not the critical path). Don't put the first check on a government timeline.**

---

### 4. SA Healthcare Sector

**Companies/entities:** Netcare, Life Healthcare, Mediclinic (private); Provincial Health Departments (public).

**AI urgency:** Real but bifurcated. Private hospitals are adopting AI (diagnostic support, patient record systems) — staff AI literacy is needed. Public hospitals are resource-constrained.

**Training budget:** Private sector has it. Public healthcare is government-budget constrained.

**Procurement speed:** Private hospital groups: moderate (6-12 weeks). Provincial health departments: government timeline (3-9 months).

**Existing programs:** Minimal for non-clinical staff. Some technical programs for clinical decision support.

**Verdict: MEDIUM priority. Good mission alignment, moderate buyer urgency, no specific market entry leverage (no equivalent of the ICASA connection). Hold for Track 3 or 4 after ICT/Finance validated.**

---

### 5. SA Education Sector

**Entities:** Department of Basic Education (DBE), SACE, private schools, NGOs doing teacher development.

**AI urgency:** High from a societal standpoint. Teachers need AI literacy. DBE has a digital transformation mandate.

**Training budget:** DBE is perennially under-resourced. Private schools have small budgets per department. Teacher development NGOs are grant-dependent.

**Procurement speed:** DBE is government procurement (3-9 months). Private schools are case-by-case.

**Verdict: STRONG mission alignment, WEAK commercial first-buyer. Education/Teachers should be the SOCIAL IMPACT track — potentially subsidized by revenue from ICT/Finance, or funded through a foundation/grant model. Not a commercial first track.**

## Track Structure Recommendation

**The Key Finding from Competitive Intelligence (Field 6):**
Prospen Africa already operates role-based tracks (AI for HR, AI for Finance, AI for Accounting, AI for Procurement). Role-based naming is NOT a differentiation point — it's table stakes in the SA market.

**What IS differentiated:** African case studies that are *sector-specific* and *documented*. An SA finance sector AI failure case study is genuinely different content from an SA government case study. Sector-based tracks allow Funda to build the authentic African case study library that competitors cannot replicate.

**Recommended track architecture:**

| Track | Build Order | Primary Buyer | Rationale |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Core Track** — AI literacy for all professionals | Build first | Any SA corporate (ICT sector first) | This is what you sell into the first pilot; role-neutral but African-professional-specific |
| **Track 1: AI Judgment for Finance Professionals** | Build second | SA banks, insurers, FSPs | FSCA/PA report creates ready-made urgency; sector-specific case studies (AI in credit, fraud, compliance) |
| **Track 2: AI Judgment for Government & Public Sector** | Build third (parallel slow burn) | ICASA, DCDT, departments | Credibility anchor; slower sale but highest legitimacy |
| **Track 3: AI Judgment for ICT & Technology Professionals** | Build after Core validates | SA telecoms, tech companies | MICT SETA; ICASA connection; closest to first pilot org |
| **Track 4: AI Literacy for Teachers** | Build last (social impact track) | NGOs, foundations, DBE | High mission; grant-fundable; not a commercial first |

**Note on naming:** "AI Judgment for..." differentiates Funda from Prospen's "Artificial Intelligence (AI) for..." naming — and signals the critical evaluation component in the name itself.

## What This Means for the Immediate Next Steps

1. **First pilot target sector: ICT/Telecoms** — leverage the ICASA connection to get a warm introduction to an ICASA licensee (MTN, Vodacom, BCX, or a mid-size ICT company). The pilot is for the **Core Track**, not a sector track.
2. **Finance Track research (Field 2/4)** should prioritize SA banking AI case studies — the FSCA/PA report is a rich source.
3. **Government/ICASA relationship** — nurture in parallel, don't bet the critical path on it.
4. **Track 4 (Teachers) is a social impact story** — worth building eventually, but with foundation/grant funding, not commercial revenue.
