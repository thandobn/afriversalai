# Funda Research Gap Analysis and Fill-In Report

## Executive Summary

After reviewing both the original landscape report and the expanded Part Two research, several major gaps remain. The biggest issue is that most findings are still based on secondary research rather than evidence from actual South African learners and buyers.

However, there are also important content gaps that can be filled immediately through additional desk research and strategic analysis.

---

# Gap 1: Economic Case Is Underdeveloped

## What Was Missing

We identified that organizations invest in AI training, but we did not fully connect AI literacy to business outcomes.

## Additional Findings

Organizations buy outcomes, not courses.

Funda's value proposition should be framed around:

- Reduced AI-related mistakes
- Better AI adoption rates
- Reduced shadow AI use
- Better policy compliance
- Increased productivity
- Better quality outputs
- Reduced reputational risk

The strongest B2B positioning is:

"Your employees are already using AI. Funda ensures they use it safely, consistently, and effectively."

---

# Gap 2: Change Management Research

## What Was Missing

Most AI initiatives fail because adoption is a people problem rather than a technology problem.

## Additional Findings

Organizations typically encounter:

### Early Enthusiasts
Use AI immediately.

### Curious Majority
Interested but uncertain.

### Skeptics
Need evidence.

### Resistant Users
Fear replacement or complexity.

Funda should build content for all four groups.

### Curriculum Implication

Add a dedicated module:

"Working With AI Without Losing Your Professional Identity"

---

# Gap 3: Manager Enablement

## What Was Missing

Managers are the primary multiplier for adoption.

## Additional Findings

Research consistently suggests:

- Teams copy managers.
- AI adoption spreads socially.
- Managers determine acceptable use.
- Managers normalize experimentation.

### Recommendation

Create a separate manager toolkit:

- Team charter template
- AI use policy starter
- Review checklist
- Adoption roadmap

---

# Gap 4: Assessment Research

## What Was Missing

The original work discussed competency but not measurement rigor.

## Additional Findings

Strong AI literacy assessment should evaluate:

1. Tool selection
2. Prompt quality
3. Verification ability
4. Risk judgment
5. Ethical reasoning
6. Workflow design

### Recommendation

Move away from multiple-choice testing.

Use:

- Scenarios
- Case studies
- Practical exercises
- Capstones

---

# Gap 5: Public Sector Track

## What Was Missing

Government was identified as a target market but not deeply explored.

## Additional Findings

Government users face unique challenges:

- Public trust
- Procurement
- Policy development
- Citizen data
- Transparency

### Suggested Government Cases

1. AI-assisted policy drafting
2. Public communication
3. Procurement evaluation
4. Citizen service chatbots
5. Record management

# Gap 8: South African Regulatory Layer

## What Was Missing

POPIA implications were not sufficiently integrated.

## Recommendation

Every learner should know:

- What personal information is
- What sensitive information is
- What can be shared with AI
- What cannot be shared
- Approval requirements

A practical "Can I Paste This?" exercise should appear in every cohort.

---

# Gap 9: Competitive Blind Spot

## What Was Missing

The analysis focused heavily on AI education competitors.

## Additional Finding

Funda also competes against:

- Doing nothing
- Internal company training
- YouTube
- Colleagues
- ChatGPT itself

The biggest competitor is actually unmanaged self-learning.

---

# Gap 10: Community Layer

## What Was Missing

The current model is course-centric.

## Additional Finding

Long-term value comes from community.

Potential assets:

- Alumni network
- Monthly AI office hours
- Sector roundtables
- Tool updates
- Case study library

# Gap 11: Research Still Required Before Design

These remain unresolved and require interviews.

## Learner Questions

- What tools are actually used?
- What mistakes are occurring?
- What fears are most common?
- What training formats work?

## Buyer Questions

- Budget ranges
- Procurement process
- Success metrics
- Reporting requirements

## Market Questions

- Existing SA AI training providers
- Pricing benchmarks
- Accreditation pathways
- B-BBEE opportunities

---

# Highest Priority Missing Research

If only one additional research phase is funded, focus on:

1. 20 learner interviews
2. 10 buyer interviews
3. Pricing analysis of South African training providers
4. Procurement analysis
5. Pilot partner identification

These five activities will create more value than another broad literature review.

---

# Final Conclusion

The largest remaining risk is not lack of research about AI literacy.

The largest remaining risk is lack of validation with actual South African learners and organizations.

The next research phase should therefore shift from:

Secondary Research → Primary Research

The strongest deliverable after this phase would be a Learning Design Brief backed by interviews, buyer conversations, pricing evidence, and pilot partner feedback.
