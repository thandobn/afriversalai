### 2. 37% Gender Penalty (African Fintech Credit Scoring)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Claim** | "37% gender penalty found in a 2025 audit of African fintech credit-scoring algorithms" |
| **Used in** | `index.html` (stat block), `resources.html` (case study), Module 2 (Concept) |
| **Source** | Dzreke, J.N. & Dzreke, P.S. (2025). "Algorithmic Bias in African Fintech Credit Scoring: A Gender Equity Audit." *Advanced Research Journal of Multidisciplinary Discoveries.* |
| **URL** | https://ar-journal.com/index.php/pub/article/view/76 |
| **Status** | VALIDATED — peer-reviewed, URL live |
| **Caveat** | Study used **synthetic audit profiles**, not real lending decisions from named lenders. It tests what algorithms *would* do, not what named lenders *did do*. The citation on `resources.html` already states this. The `index.html` stat block does not carry the caveat — acceptable for a homepage hook, but be precise in B2B conversations. |
| **If challenged** | "The study used a synthetic audit methodology — standard practice in algorithmic bias research because real lenders won't open their models. It reveals what the algorithms produce when exposed to matched profiles that differ only by gender. The 37% figure is the size of that gap across 10 systems." |

### 6. 67% / 32% / 74% Corporate GenAI Adoption (Module 7 — Corporate)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Claim** | "67% of surveyed SA enterprises reported active GenAI use; 32% reported shadow AI (unapproved); 74% used Microsoft Copilot" |
| **Used in** | `module-7-corporate.html` |
| **Source** | World Wide Worx, *South African Generative AI Roadmap 2025* (with Dell Technologies and Intel). Survey of 100+ mid-sized and large SA enterprises. |
| **URL** | https://www.worldwideworx.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/The-SA-Gen-AI-Roadmap-2025.pdf · Coverage: https://mybroadband.co.za/news/ai/603022-ai-warning-for-businesses-in-south-africa.html |
| **Status** | VALIDATED — with correct framing (see caveats) |
| **Caveats** | (1) These are **organisation-level figures** (surveyed businesses), NOT individual employee usage rates. The original module copy said "74% of SA corporate employees" — this was wrong and has been corrected. (2) 74% = SA enterprises using Copilot. 67% = enterprises reporting active GenAI use overall. 32% = enterprises reporting unofficial/unapproved use. 93% reported ChatGPT use (paid or free). (3) The governance-lag story (adoption outpacing policy) is the Afriversal-relevant angle — not "ChatGPT is the winner." |
| **If challenged** | "These figures are from World Wide Worx's 2025 survey of South African enterprises. They describe organisational adoption patterns, not individual employee usage. The relevant insight is that most organisations are using GenAI faster than they have built the policy or judgment frameworks to govern it safely — which is exactly the gap Afriversal addresses." |
