# Funda — Vision Document

> Working name: Funda (Ndebele/Zulu: "to learn"). Not a final brand name.
> Last updated: 2026-06-06

---

## The Gap We're Filling

Robust AI learning resources exist for US and European learners — but almost nothing purpose-built for African learners, African contexts, and African realities. What exists either treats Africa as an afterthought ("add a local case study") or is simply inaccessible: designed for desktop, high-bandwidth, high-literacy environments that most African learners don't have.

The demand is real. The AU AI Strategy identifies skills and AI literacy as a core pillar. Companies and governments across Africa are actively deploying AI tools into their operations — and their staff have no structured way to engage with those tools critically and safely.

Funda is the answer to that gap.

---

## What Funda Is

An AI education initiative building ethical, grounded, African-centered AI literacy courses — designed for how African learners actually live and work, not how Silicon Valley imagines they do.

**Not "here's how to use ChatGPT."**
Real AI literacy: understand what AI is, what it can and can't do, when to use it and when not to, how to evaluate its outputs, and how to stay in control of decisions that matter.

---

## Core Principles

**1. African-centered, not Western AI courses with African branding.**
Examples, case studies, and problems start from African professional contexts. The curriculum takes African ways of knowing seriously — not as a nice-to-have, but as the starting point. African educators and content reviewers must be part of the design process, not consulted after the fact.

**2. Problem-posing, not banking (Freire).**
Learners are not empty vessels to fill with AI knowledge. The curriculum starts from their questions, their problems, their realities. Learners and facilitators think together.

**3. Evaluate and critique, not just use (Critical Digital Pedagogy).**
AI systems embed assumptions from the cultures that built them. Funda graduates can see this. They know how to question AI outputs, identify when AI is the wrong tool, and make decisions that aren't just deferred to the model.

**4. Built for expansion from day one.**
Phase 1 serves corporate and government clients in office environments — computers, broadband, standard infrastructure. But Funda is designed so that expanding to mobile, low-bandwidth, and offline contexts doesn't require rebuilding from scratch. Content is modular, platform choices preserve flexibility, and the door to broader access stays open.

**5. Explicit inclusion — not as an afterthought.**
Without explicit targets and design features, programs default to serving male, urban, already-connected learners. Funda designs for women, rural communities, and those with lower prior digital exposure from the start.

**6. Experiential, not passive.**
Learning happens by doing. Every module follows a concrete experience → reflection → theory → experiment cycle. No pure lecture tracks.

## Who We Serve

### Phase 1 Geography: South Africa

Funda's Phase 1 market is South Africa. Reasons:
- South Africa has the most mature AI policy framework in Sub-Saharan Africa (DCDT AI Policy Framework, approved by Cabinet March 2026).
- B-BBEE legislation requires companies to invest in training — creating mandatory demand that Funda can serve.
- SA has established ICT and corporate sectors with existing training budgets and procurement processes.
- Initial market entry is through existing professional networks in the South African ICT sector.

Phase 2 expands to other anglophone African markets (Kenya, Ghana, Nigeria, Zimbabwe) based on what Phase 1 proves.

### Primary (B2B Revenue Engine)
Working professionals, corporate teams, and government staff. Companies and governments are the paying customers — they purchase training for their people. This is the sustainable model that doesn't depend on grants.

Target payers: HR and L&D departments at mid-to-large South African companies; government ministries with digital transformation mandates (DCDT, DPSA, National Treasury); NGOs with operational AI adoption. B-BBEE compliance makes training spend a procurement priority, not a discretionary one.

Target learners: Staff at those organizations — often skilled professionals (finance, operations, communications, HR) who know their domain deeply but have had no structured engagement with AI tools. **Not** developer-track learners.

### Secondary / Long-Term
Broader public accessibility. The vision is that anyone should eventually be able to access foundational Funda content regardless of whether their employer is paying. This comes after the B2B engine is running.

## Course Structure

```
Core Track (accessible to all)
├── What is AI — and what isn't
├── How to evaluate AI outputs
├── Where AI helps and where it doesn't
├── AI ethics and bias in practice
└── Staying in control of AI-assisted decisions

Specialized Tracks (corporate/government)
├── AI in operations (process automation, decision support)
├── AI for data and reporting
├── AI policy and governance for organizations
└── AI for [sector] — customized by industry
```

## What a Funda Graduate Can Do

After completing the core track, a graduate can:
- Explain what AI systems do and don't do, without jargon
- Evaluate whether an AI-generated output is reliable for a given purpose
- Identify at least two ways an AI system might produce biased or wrong outputs in their professional context
- Decide when to use AI, when to use a human, and when to use neither
- Raise a meaningful concern about an AI deployment in their organization

These are Bloom's Apply and Evaluate level outcomes — not just recall and recognition.

## What Funda Is NOT

- Not a Western AI course translated to Swahili
- Not a tool tutorial ("here's how to use Gemini in 5 steps")
- Not a certification factory with high completion, low competency
- Not a donor-dependent grant project with no sustainability path
- Not built exclusively for Western professional contexts — African realities, not Western assumptions, shape the curriculum

## Mission Integrity Policy

Funda's core promise — teaching judgment, critical evaluation, and AI literacy rather than tool tutorials — is not a product feature. It is the product. The following are non-negotiable red lines that apply to every client engagement, regardless of contract size:

**Funda will not:**
- Strip or significantly minimize the critical evaluation / AI judgment components from any course at a client's request
- Deliver training that is primarily tool-tutorial in nature (step-by-step walkthroughs of specific products) regardless of how the request is framed
- Issue certificates for completion-only programs with no competency assessment
- Allow a client's brand to replace Funda's without disclosing the nature of the program to learners

**Funda will:**
- Customize sector-specific examples, case studies, and context (this is encouraged and expected)
- Work with clients to adapt pacing, delivery format, and facilitation approach to their environment
- Decline or renegotiate engagements where the client's requirements conflict with the above

**Why this policy exists:** The first corporate client who asks Funda to remove the "critical thinking" content and just teach Copilot shortcuts is not a malicious actor — they're a normal L&D buyer trying to hit a quick win. Without a documented red line, the answer becomes a negotiation. With it, the answer is clear. Every compromise on this policy erodes the one thing that makes Funda worth paying for.

## Competitive Moat Strategy

Differentiation alone is not a moat. Funda's differentiation (African context + critical thinking + Freire pedagogy) can be replicated by a well-resourced competitor adding one module. The following moat-building strategy creates position that is harder to imitate:

**1. Government alignment (hardest to replicate)**
Pursue formal alignment with the DCDT's National AI Policy Framework. A Funda program endorsed or referenced in national AI literacy efforts is a moat that Google and Microsoft cannot buy easily. Government ministries as pilot partners or co-designers create legitimacy that compounds.

**2. SETA accreditation pathway**
SETA-accredited programs allow employers to recover 40–60% of training costs through discretionary grants. This dramatically improves Funda's price-to-value ratio for corporate buyers. Accreditation takes 6–12 months — begin the process after the first pilot proves the curriculum.

**3. Proprietary African case study library**
No competitor has case studies drawn from real African professional contexts across multiple sectors. Every session run with real corporate learners generates new material. This library compounds — and is invisible to competitors.

**4. Alumni and facilitator network**
Funda graduates who become in-organization advocates, and African facilitators trained in the Funda methodology, create distribution that doesn't require Funda to sell to every client. This is the local trainer / Master Trainer model — cheaper to scale, more culturally resonant, harder to replicate.

**5. Reputation for rigor**
A Funda certificate that is known to mean demonstrated competency (not just completion) becomes more valuable every year. This is a compounding asset. Reputation for rigor takes time to build but is virtually impossible to buy.

## Strategic Bets

1. **B2B first.** Employers will pay for structured AI training in a way individual learners won't. Corporate and government contracts are the revenue engine that funds broader accessibility.

2. **South Africa first.** One market, done well, proves the model and builds the evidence base before Pan-African expansion.

3. **Quality over scale, early.** Build one excellent core track and prove the model with real learners before expanding to specialized tracks or additional geographies.

4. **B-BBEE alignment.** South African companies are legally required to invest in skills development and report against B-BBEE scorecards. SETA-accredited training allows cost recovery. Positioning Funda within the B-BBEE compliance ecosystem creates a structural demand driver, not just a persuasion problem.

5. **Local facilitators, not external instructors.** The Master Trainer model (from STRYDE evidence) — train facilitators from within communities and organizations — is more sustainable and culturally resonant than importing external instructors.

6. **Government partnership early.** Programs embedded in policy frameworks survive funding cycles. The DCDT's National AI Policy Framework (approved March 2026) identifies skills capacity as Pillar 1. Funda should engage DCDT as a partner and potential co-endorser, not just a regulatory backdrop.

## What "Done" Eventually Looks Like

- A thriving B2B training practice with 3+ country reach (SA → anglophone Africa)
- SETA-accredited core track that corporate buyers can fund through skills levy recovery
- A core track that any African professional can access (employer-sponsored or self-directed)
- Local facilitators running courses in-country with Funda materials and support
- A reputation for rigor: Funda graduates are meaningfully more capable, not just certified
