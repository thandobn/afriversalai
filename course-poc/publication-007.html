Part A
General

1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (“AUP”) establishes the rules governing access to and use of AfriversalAI’s learning platform, Corporate Portal, assessments, certifications, digital credentials and related services, to protect learners, customers, partners and AfriversalAI while ensuring the integrity of all learning and certification activities.

2. Scope & Who It Applies To

This Policy applies to Customers, Learners, Programme Administrators, Facilitators, Contractors, Referral Partners, Enterprise Partners and any person authorised to access AfriversalAI systems or services. Compliance is a condition of access to the Services.

3. Definitions

Capitalised terms have the meanings given in the Master Services Agreement (Publication 010).

Part B
Acceptable Use

4. Permitted Use

Users may access the Services solely for legitimate educational, administrative or business purposes authorised under the applicable agreement, including participating in programmes, attending live sessions, completing assessments, downloading authorised resources, viewing authorised reports, contacting support, and using certificates in accordance with this Policy.

5. User Accounts

Each authorised user maintains accurate account information, keeps credentials confidential, promptly notifies AfriversalAI of suspected unauthorised access, and does not share accounts. Each learner account is for a single authorised individual.

6. Access Codes

Access Codes are issued to authorised customers following confirmed payment, are intended only for the authorised cohort, may not be sold, transferred, shared or reused without written approval, and may be suspended or revoked for misuse.

7. Learning Platform & Corporate Portal

Users shall use the platform responsibly and shall not interfere with operations, attempt unauthorised access, upload malicious code, disrupt other users, or reverse-engineer or probe platform security.

Part C
Content & Intellectual Property

8. Learning Materials

All learning content remains AfriversalAI’s intellectual property. Except where expressly authorised, users may not copy, reproduce, distribute, modify, publish, sell, sublicense or create derivative works from AfriversalAI materials. Limited downloading or printing may be permitted only where expressly authorised for personal learning.

9. Assessments

To preserve certification integrity, users shall not impersonate another learner, submit work on behalf of another person, disclose assessment questions publicly, share answer keys, or attempt to manipulate assessment outcomes. AfriversalAI may investigate suspected misconduct under the Certification & Assessment Policy.

10. Certificates & Digital Credentials

Certificates and digital credentials remain subject to verification, may not be altered or presented misleadingly, and may not be used after revocation. AfriversalAI may revoke certifications obtained through fraud, misconduct or material breach.

11. Artificial Intelligence Tools

The Programme encourages responsible AI use. Users apply independent judgement, critically evaluate AI outputs, comply with organisational AI policies, and protect confidential and personal information. Where assessments permit AI-assisted work, learners remain responsible for accuracy, originality and integrity.

Part D
Prohibited Activities

12. Prohibited Activities

Users shall not misuse platform resources; upload unlawful, offensive or malicious content; bypass security controls; infringe IP rights; harass users; interfere with assessments; misrepresent certification status; or use the Services for unlawful purposes.

13. Academic Integrity

Misconduct includes plagiarism, impersonation, falsification of records, unauthorised collaboration where individual work is required, and deliberate misuse of AI-generated content in breach of assessment requirements. Breaches may result in assessment failure, suspension, certificate revocation, removal from the Programme, and other permitted actions.

14. Information Security

Users use strong passwords, secure their devices, report suspected incidents promptly, and protect confidential information accessed through the Services.

15. Fraud & Misrepresentation

Users shall not falsify identities, create fraudulent accounts, misuse Member IDs, alter certificates, falsely claim AfriversalAI accreditation or endorsement, or represent themselves as authorised representatives of AfriversalAI without written authority.

16. Monitoring

To protect integrity, security and operation, AfriversalAI may monitor platform usage, authentication events, assessment activity and system performance, subject to applicable law and the Data Processing & Privacy Addendum.

Part E
Enforcement

17. Suspension

AfriversalAI may suspend or restrict access where reasonably necessary, including for suspected fraud, misuse of Access Codes, security risks, non-payment, material breach, or significant policy violation. Where practicable, the affected Customer is informed of the reason.

18. Investigations

AfriversalAI may investigate suspected breaches; users are expected to cooperate with reasonable requests. Appropriate records may be retained to support the investigation and any resulting actions.

19. Reporting Concerns

Suspected misuse, fraud, security incidents or policy violations should be reported promptly to AfriversalAI at ask@afriversal.ai.

20. Policy Updates

AfriversalAI may update this Policy to reflect changes in law, technology, security practices or the Services. Material updates apply prospectively and Customers are notified through the Corporate Portal or agreed channels.
