Enterprise Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

MNDA — Confidentiality between AfriversalAI and the Receiving Party
Publication AAI-P005Official PublicationVersion 1.0Confidential

This Enterprise Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (“Agreement”) establishes the obligations of confidentiality between AfriversalAI (Pty) Ltd and the other party for the purpose of evaluating, discussing, negotiating, implementing, or delivering products, services, partnerships, procurement opportunities, research, consulting engagements, or any other legitimate business relationship. The parties acknowledge that confidential information is a valuable business asset and agree to protect it in accordance with this Agreement.

2. Purpose of Disclosure

The Parties wish to exchange confidential information solely for purposes that may include:

  • evaluation of products or services;
  • enterprise AI transformation projects;
  • procurement activities;
  • training and workforce enablement programmes;
  • technology demonstrations;
  • proof-of-concept projects;
  • commercial negotiations;
  • strategic partnerships;
  • reseller or referral discussions;
  • consulting engagements;
  • due diligence;
  • investment discussions; and
  • any other mutually agreed business purpose.

No licence, ownership right, intellectual property interest, or commercial right is granted merely by disclosing Confidential Information.

3. Definition of Confidential Information

“Confidential Information” means all information disclosed by one Party to the other, whether orally, visually, electronically, digitally, or in writing, that is confidential by its nature or would reasonably be understood to be confidential. This includes, without limitation: business strategies; pricing models; customer information; supplier information; training methodologies; the Funda Five™ framework; curriculum designs; assessments; examination material; AI prompts; software; platform architecture; source code; documentation; financial information; marketing strategies; business plans; proposals; research; intellectual property; security procedures; authentication methods; unpublished materials; learner information; certification records; and any information designated as confidential. Information remains confidential regardless of the medium in which it is stored or communicated.

4. Excluded Information

Information is not Confidential Information where the receiving Party can demonstrate that it: was lawfully known before disclosure; becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement; is independently developed without use of the disclosed information; is lawfully received from a third party without confidentiality obligations; or is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order.

5. Obligations of the Receiving Party

The receiving Party shall:

  • keep Confidential Information strictly confidential;
  • use Confidential Information only for the agreed business purpose;
  • exercise at least the same degree of care it applies to its own confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable care;
  • restrict access to employees, contractors, advisers or representatives who have a legitimate need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement;
  • not copy, reproduce, publish, distribute or disclose Confidential Information except as permitted under this Agreement;
  • promptly notify the disclosing Party of any suspected unauthorised disclosure, access or misuse; and
  • cooperate in mitigating any resulting harm.

6. Intellectual Property

All Confidential Information remains the exclusive property of the disclosing Party. Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of intellectual property, copyright, patents, trademarks, methodologies, software, training materials, certification systems, business processes or proprietary know-how. Any licence to use such material must be granted separately in writing.

7. Standard of Care

Each Party shall implement reasonable administrative, technical and organisational safeguards to protect Confidential Information from unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration or destruction.

8. Return or Destruction of Information

Upon written request, or upon termination of discussions where appropriate, the receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy Confidential Information in its possession, except where retention is required by law or for legitimate record-keeping purposes.

9. No Public Announcements

Neither Party shall issue press releases, marketing material, public statements or announcements concerning the existence or subject matter of discussions without the prior written consent of the other Party.

10. Term

This Agreement becomes effective on the Effective Date and remains in force for three (3) years unless terminated earlier by mutual written agreement. The confidentiality obligations survive termination for five (5) years from the date of the last disclosure, or for as long as applicable law requires in respect of personal information and trade secrets.

11. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by and interpreted in accordance with the laws of the Republic of South Africa.

12. Electronic Signatures

The Parties agree that electronic signatures and electronic acceptance are legally binding and have the same force and effect as handwritten signatures, in accordance with the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002.

13. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties regarding confidentiality and supersedes all prior discussions, negotiations or understandings relating to its subject matter.

14. Signatures

By signing below, the Parties agree to be bound by this Agreement. Signing electronically is legally binding under the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002.
