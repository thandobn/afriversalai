Part A
Introduction

1. Purpose

This Data Processing & Privacy Addendum (“DPA”) forms part of the Enterprise Master Services Agreement and establishes the obligations of the Parties regarding the processing of Personal Information in connection with AfriversalAI’s enterprise learning services. The Parties intend that all processing of Personal Information be undertaken lawfully, fairly, securely and transparently, in accordance with applicable data-protection legislation.

2. Scope

This DPA applies whenever AfriversalAI processes Personal Information relating to learners, programme administrators, customer representatives, facilitators, executive sponsors or other authorised users in connection with the Services.

3. Applicable Legislation

Processing complies with applicable legislation, including where relevant: the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA); the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act; the Promotion of Access to Information Act; and any applicable data-protection legislation in jurisdictions where Services are delivered.

Part B
Personal Information

5. Categories of Personal Information

Examples include: name; surname; business email; business telephone; department; job title; employee number; organisation; learning progress; assessment results; certificate number; Member ID. Only information reasonably necessary for delivery of the Services is processed.

6. Processing Activities

AfriversalAI may process Personal Information for: learner registration; authentication; Access Code management; programme delivery; assessments; certification; reporting; customer support; audit records; compliance; and security.

7. Special Personal Information

AfriversalAI does not intentionally require Special Personal Information for ordinary programme delivery. If it becomes necessary for a particular engagement, the Parties will ensure an appropriate lawful basis exists before processing.

8. Cross-Border Processing

Where Personal Information is transferred across national borders, AfriversalAI takes reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place and that applicable legal requirements are observed.

Part C
Security & Lifecycle

9. Security Measures

AfriversalAI maintains reasonable administrative, organisational and technical safeguards designed to protect Personal Information against unauthorised access, accidental disclosure, alteration, destruction and misuse. Safeguards may include access controls, authentication, encryption where appropriate, secure hosting, monitoring and staff confidentiality obligations.

10. Access Control

Access is limited to authorised personnel who require it to perform their responsibilities, with appropriate authentication and role-based permissions.

11. Retention

Personal Information is retained only for as long as reasonably necessary to deliver the Services, maintain certification records, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes or enforce contractual rights. Retention periods may differ by record type and legal requirement.

12. Deletion

Subject to legal or contractual obligations, Personal Information is securely deleted or anonymised following expiry of the applicable retention period or on a valid Customer request.

13. Security Incidents & Breaches

Where AfriversalAI becomes aware of a Personal Information security incident affecting Customer data, it will investigate and, where required by law or contract, notify the Customer without undue delay, including where reasonably available a description of the incident, the categories of information affected, the likely impact, and actions taken or proposed.

Part D
Responsibilities & Rights

14. Customer Responsibilities

The Customer is responsible for ensuring it has an appropriate lawful basis to provide learner information; ensuring Personal Information supplied is accurate; informing learners where required; obtaining any necessary permissions or consents; and maintaining the security of its own systems.

15. AfriversalAI Responsibilities

AfriversalAI will process Personal Information only for authorised purposes; implement reasonable security measures; ensure personnel are subject to confidentiality obligations; cooperate with reasonable data-protection requests; and assist, where appropriate, with lawful requests from data subjects.

16. Learner Rights

Where applicable under law, learners may have rights to access, correction, deletion (where legally permissible), objection to certain processing, and complaint to the relevant regulator. Requests are directed through the appropriate Customer contact or AfriversalAI privacy contact.

17. Subprocessors

AfriversalAI may engage trusted third-party service providers to support delivery, and remains responsible for ensuring such subprocessors are bound by appropriate confidentiality and data-protection obligations.

18. Confidentiality

Any person authorised to process Personal Information on behalf of AfriversalAI is subject to appropriate confidentiality obligations.

Part E
Legal

19. Liability

Each Party remains responsible for its own compliance with applicable data-protection laws. Nothing in this DPA limits liability where such limitation would be prohibited by law.

20. Audit & Compliance

Upon reasonable notice and where appropriate to verify compliance, the Parties may cooperate in responding to reasonable information requests relating to data-protection practices, subject to confidentiality, security and legal restrictions.

21. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa unless otherwise agreed in writing.
