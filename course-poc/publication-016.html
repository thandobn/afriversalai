Part A
Certification Framework

1. Purpose

This Policy establishes the standards governing assessments, certification, digital credentials, academic integrity, verification and record management for all AfriversalAI learning programmes, ensuring that every certificate represents demonstrated competence supported by consistent assessment and quality assurance.

2. Scope

Applies to learners, enterprise customers, programme administrators, facilitators, assessors, moderators, certification administrators and authorised partners involved in programme delivery.

3. Guiding Principles

  • Competence is demonstrated rather than assumed.
  • Assessment supports learning as well as evaluation.
  • Responsible use of AI is encouraged within clearly defined boundaries.
  • Academic integrity underpins the credibility of every credential.
  • Certification standards are applied consistently across all cohorts.

4. Certification Framework

AfriversalAI may award certifications at different levels: AI Literacy Foundations; AI Practitioner; AI Governance; AI Leadership; and specialist or sector-specific certifications. Programme-specific requirements are described in the applicable curriculum documentation.

Part B
Assessment

5. Assessment Philosophy

Assessments evaluate whether learners can apply knowledge and judgement in practical workplace contexts. Methods may include knowledge checks, quizzes, case studies, practical exercises, reflective activities, scenario-based assessments, facilitator observations and other approved methods.

6. Assessment Methods

Each programme specifies the assessment format, number of opportunities, weighting (where applicable), completion requirements and any mandatory live participation. Instructions are communicated before the assessment.

7. Pass Requirements

The pass mark is published in the relevant curriculum or Commercial Schedule. Certification requires completion of mandatory learning activities, attendance at required live sessions (where applicable), successful completion of required assessments, and compliance with the Academic Integrity requirements of this Policy.

8. Reassessment

Where permitted, learners who do not achieve the required standard may be offered reassessment in accordance with programme rules. Additional fees may apply where stated in the Commercial Schedule.

9. Reasonable Adjustments

AfriversalAI will consider reasonable adjustments to assessment arrangements where appropriate and consistent with maintaining integrity and learning outcomes. Requests should be submitted before the scheduled assessment.

Part C
Certification

10. Award of Certification

Certification is awarded only after verification that all programme requirements are satisfied. Certificates are issued digitally unless otherwise agreed.

11. Digital Certificates

Each certificate may include learner name; programme title; certification level; completion date; unique certificate number; Member ID; verification method (QR code or verification portal); and authorised signatures. Certificates remain AfriversalAI’s intellectual property while recognising the learner’s achievement.

12. Member IDs

Each learner is issued a unique AfriversalAI Member ID that links to their certification history, supports verification, remains unique to the learner, and may be used across future AfriversalAI programmes.

13. Certificate Verification

AfriversalAI maintains a verification process enabling authorised employers, customers or third parties to confirm certificate authenticity, programme completed, completion date and certification status, subject to applicable privacy obligations.

14. Replacement Certificates

Replacement certificates may be issued following identity verification. An administrative fee may apply.

Part D
Academic Integrity

15. Learner Responsibilities

Learners complete their own work, comply with assessment instructions, protect assessment materials, use AI responsibly where permitted, and report suspected misconduct.

16. Responsible Use of AI

Where AI-assisted work is permitted, learners remain responsible for the accuracy of submitted work, exercising independent judgement, acknowledging AI assistance where required, and ensuring confidential or personal information is not improperly disclosed to AI systems. Programme-specific instructions may place additional limits.

17. Academic Misconduct

Examples: plagiarism; impersonation; unauthorised collaboration; falsification of records; misuse of AI contrary to assessment requirements; sharing assessment content or answer keys; attempting to obtain certification fraudulently.

18. Investigations

Suspected misconduct is investigated fairly and impartially. The learner has an opportunity to respond before any decision. Records are retained per the applicable retention policy.

19. Appeals

Learners may appeal assessment or certification decisions in writing within the specified timeframe. Appeals are reviewed by personnel not directly involved in the original decision wherever practicable.

Part E
Records & Governance

20. Record Management

AfriversalAI maintains records relating to enrolment, assessments, certifications, Member IDs, verification requests and appeals, retained per applicable legal requirements and internal retention policies.

21. Certificate Revocation

AfriversalAI may suspend or revoke a certificate where there is evidence of fraud, identity misrepresentation, academic misconduct, material administrative error, or breach of applicable agreements or policies affecting credential integrity. Where practicable, the learner is informed and given an opportunity to respond before a final decision.

22. Quality Assurance

Quality assurance may include assessment moderation, periodic curriculum review, facilitator development, assessment analysis, learner feedback and programme review — supporting consistency and continuous improvement.

23. Continuous Improvement

AfriversalAI periodically reviews this Policy, assessment methodologies and certification standards to reflect developments in AI, learning science, regulation and industry practice, while maintaining credential integrity. Certification governance is overseen by the Academic & Certification Committee.
