2. Purpose & Principles

AfriversalAI provides enterprise AI capability development services to organisations seeking to build AI literacy, governance capability, responsible AI adoption, and workforce transformation across their employees. This Agreement establishes the legal framework governing all products, services, subscriptions, learning programmes, certifications, consulting engagements, workshops, executive briefings, implementation services and enterprise learning solutions delivered by AfriversalAI. Unless expressly agreed otherwise in writing, this Agreement governs every Order placed by the Customer.

AfriversalAI delivers its services according to five enterprise principles:

Principle 1. AI should improve human judgement — not replace it.
Principle 2. Every learner should understand how AI learns, reasons, fails, and should be questioned.
Principle 3. Certification represents demonstrated competence rather than simple attendance.
Principle 4. Enterprise AI capability requires continual learning as technology evolves.
Principle 5. The AfriversalAI methodology is proprietary intellectual property.

3. Definitions

In this Agreement, unless the context indicates otherwise, each defined term has a single authoritative meaning used consistently across the publication suite:

Access Code — a unique, single-use enrolment code entitling one Learner to enrol in a Cohort.
Assessment — any test, task or evaluation used to determine competence.
Business Day — any day other than a Saturday, Sunday or South African public holiday.
Certificate / Digital Credential — a credential confirming a Learner is assessed as demonstrated.
Cohort — a group of Learners enrolled under a common Access Code and intake.
Commercial Schedule — Publication 011, recording pricing and commercial particulars.
Confidential Information — non-public information of a Party disclosed under this Agreement.
Corporate Portal — the secure environment for managing the Customer’s programme, contracts, invoices and reporting.
Customer Data — data provided by the Customer, including Learner details.
Effective Date — the date of last signature on the Signature Page.
Enterprise Licence — the limited licence granted under clause 19.
Facilitator — a person delivering Programme sessions for AfriversalAI.
Fees — the amounts payable under the Commercial Schedule.
Funda Five™ — AfriversalAI’s proprietary framework (Task → Data → Tool → Trust → Human).
Learner — an employee or authorised individual of the Customer enrolled in the Programme.
Learning Platform — the online systems through which the Programme is delivered.
Member ID — the cohort-stamped unique identifier issued to each Learner.
Programme — the AI-literacy and enablement programme and related materials.
Services — the services described in clause 5 and any applicable Statement of Work.
Statement of Work / SOW — Publication 012, describing a specific engagement.
Support Services — the support described in clause 10.

4. Interpretation

Headings are for convenience only. Words importing the singular include the plural and vice versa. References to legislation include amendments. “Including” means “including without limitation”. This Agreement is not to be construed against the drafter.

Part B
The Services

5. Scope of Services

AfriversalAI may provide one or more of the following under this Agreement: AI literacy learning programmes; executive education; workforce transformation programmes; AI governance programmes; AI strategy workshops; sector-specific learning pathways; instructor-led training; virtual learning events; enterprise webinars; executive briefings; consulting services; assessments; certification; learning analytics; digital credentialing; enterprise reporting; implementation support; and other professional services agreed in writing. The precise Services for an engagement are set out in the applicable Commercial Schedule, Statement of Work or accepted Order.

6. Programme Delivery

AfriversalAI delivers the Services with reasonable skill and care and in accordance with any Service Level Schedule. Delivery may be self-paced, virtual-live, classroom or hybrid. AfriversalAI may schedule reasonable maintenance and may substitute Facilitators of equivalent competence.

7. Professional Services

Consulting, advisory and implementation services are provided as described in the applicable Statement of Work, on a time-and-materials or fixed-fee basis as agreed.

8. Learning Platform

AfriversalAI provides access to the Learning Platform for the licensed term. AfriversalAI may update, improve or modify the Platform provided that it does not materially reduce core functionality during the term.

9. Corporate Portal

The Customer is provided secure Corporate Portal access to manage its programme, contracts, invoices, proof of payment, downloads and reporting, subject to clause 24.

10. Support Services

AfriversalAI provides reasonable Learner and administrator support during Business Days, as further described in the Service Level Schedule (Publication 013) where applicable.

Part C
Commercial Terms

11. Orders

Each engagement is constituted by three documents: this Master Services Agreement (the legal framework), the Commercial Schedule (the commercial details), and the Statement of Work (the implementation). The Customer places Orders by signing a Commercial Schedule or issuing a Purchase Order referencing this Agreement.

12. Statements of Work

Individual projects, cohorts or workshops may be documented in a Statement of Work (Publication 012), which incorporates and is governed by this Agreement and is effective when signed by both Parties or on AfriversalAI’s acceptance of a valid Purchase Order.

13. Commercial Schedule

All pricing and commercial particulars are set out in the Commercial Schedule (Publication 011). No pricing is contained in this Agreement.

14. Fees

The Customer pays the Fees set out in the Commercial Schedule. Fees are exclusive of VAT.

15. Taxes

All amounts are exclusive of taxes, duties and withholdings, which are for the Customer’s account, save for taxes on AfriversalAI’s income.

16. Payment

Payment is made by electronic funds transfer to AfriversalAI’s nominated account using the invoice number as reference, and proof of payment is uploaded to the Corporate Portal. Access Codes, enrolment and Certificates are released only on receipt of cleared funds unless otherwise agreed.

17. Late Payments

Overdue amounts may bear interest at the maximum rate permitted by law, and AfriversalAI may recover reasonable collection costs.

18. Suspension

AfriversalAI may suspend Portal access, Access Codes, Certificates, support and reporting for overdue accounts, on reasonable notice where practicable.

Part D
Customer Use

19. Enterprise Licence

The Customer purchases a limited licence to access the AfriversalAI Services. The Customer does not purchase ownership, copyright, source material, methodologies, certification systems, assessment content, course architecture or any intellectual property. The licence is non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable in accordance with this Agreement, and limited to the number of purchased Learner seats.

20. Access Codes

Each Access Code is unique, single-use, non-transferable, may expire, and may be revoked for misuse or non-payment. Access Codes may not be shared, sold or reused.

21. Learner Registration

Learners register with an Access Code, are grouped into the Customer’s Cohort, and are issued a Member ID. Enrolments may not exceed purchased seats without a further Order.

22. Customer Responsibilities

The Customer nominates a Programme Administrator, provides accurate Learner information, pays invoices, ensures Learner participation and suitable access, maintains account security, does not share Access Codes, and complies with the Acceptable Use Policy.

23. Programme Administrator

The Customer maintains a nominated Programme Administrator responsible for managing its Portal, Learners and reporting, and for the confidentiality of administrator credentials.

24. Acceptable Use

The Customer and its Learners will use the Services in accordance with the Acceptable Use Policy (Publication 015) and will not misuse, probe, or attempt to circumvent the Learning Platform.

Part E
Certification

25. Assessments

Assessments determine Learner competence. AfriversalAI sets and may vary assessment methods and pass marks.

26. Certification

Certificates are issued only to Learners assessed as demonstrated and carried against a verifiable Member ID.

27. Digital Credentials

Certificates may be issued as verifiable digital credentials carrying the Learner’s Member ID.

28. Verification

AfriversalAI provides digital verification of Certificates and may withhold, revoke or replace credentials where integrity is compromised.

29. Academic Integrity

Fraud, impersonation or misrepresentation in Assessments may result in revocation of Certificates and suspension of access, without refund.

Part F
Ownership

30. Intellectual Property

All intellectual property in and to the Programme and the AfriversalAI ecosystem is and remains the exclusive property of AfriversalAI, including logos and brand, frameworks and methodologies, slides, videos, books, graphics, assessments, reports, dashboards, learning architecture, AI prompts, the certification system and Member IDs.

31. Proprietary Methodologies

AfriversalAI’s methodologies are proprietary and confidential. No right to reproduce or repurpose them is granted except as expressly licensed.

32. Funda Five™

The Funda Five™ framework is a proprietary methodology of AfriversalAI. The Customer and its Learners receive a limited licence to use it for internal learning only and acquire no ownership of it.

33. AI Literacy Framework

AfriversalAI’s AI-literacy competency framework and assessment criteria are proprietary and remain owned by AfriversalAI.

34. Learning Content

All learning content is licensed, not sold, and may not be copied, duplicated, shared, resold or used to build a competing offering.

35. Customer Data

The Customer retains ownership of Customer Data. AfriversalAI processes it to deliver the Services and provide reporting, subject to clause 41.

36. Learner Records

AfriversalAI owns and maintains Member IDs, certification records, assessment history, digital transcripts, learning analytics and verification records generated through the Programme. The Customer receives reporting in respect of its Learners.

37. Enterprise Customer Ownership

  1. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, AfriversalAI remains the contracting supplier of the Services delivered under this Agreement.
  2. Customer relationships, learner records, certification records, programme history, Member IDs and associated learning data remain administered within the AfriversalAI platform and ecosystem.
  3. Where a customer is introduced by a referral partner, reseller, implementation partner or strategic alliance, that introduction does not by itself transfer ownership of the customer relationship or the right to represent AfriversalAI.
  4. Any exclusive customer-management arrangement or transfer of commercial rights must be documented in a separate written agreement signed by authorised representatives of AfriversalAI.

38. Referral Customers

Where the Customer is introduced by a Partner, the Customer remains a customer of AfriversalAI. A Partner may not replace AfriversalAI as the contracting supplier or assume ownership of Learner records or certification.

39. Channel Partners

Resellers, implementation partners and strategic alliances operate under separate written terms (Publication 017) and do not acquire rights in the AfriversalAI ecosystem beyond those expressly granted.

Part G
Risk

40. Confidentiality

Each Party keeps the other’s Confidential Information confidential, uses it only for the purposes of this Agreement, and protects it with reasonable care, subter to disclosures required by law. These obligations survive for 5 years, and indefinitely for trade secrets.

41. POPIA & Data Protection

Each Party complies with POPIA. Detailed terms are in the Data Processing Addendum (Publication 014). AfriversalAI processes Learner personal information to deliver the Programme, issue Member IDs and Certificates and report to the Customer. The Customer warrants it has a lawful basis to share Learner personal information.

42. Information Security

AfriversalAI applies reasonable technical and organisational measures including access controls, encryption in transit and access logging.

43. Warranties

AfriversalAI warrants it will provide the Services with reasonable skill and care. Except as expressly stated, the Services are provided “as is” without further warranty. The Customer warrants the accuracy of information it provides and its signatory’s authority.

44. Indemnities

The Customer indemnifies AfriversalAI against claims arising from the Customer’s breach, misuse of the Programme or Access Codes, or unlawful processing of personal information.

45. Limitation of Liability

Neither Party is liable to the other for indirect, incidental, consequential, special, exemplary or punitive damages, including loss of profits, revenue, business opportunity or goodwill, whether in contract, delict (tort), statute or otherwise. Subject to applicable law, AfriversalAI’s aggregate liability arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed the total Fees paid by the Customer under the applicable Statement of Work giving rise to the claim during the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to liability.

46. Insurance

AfriversalAI maintains reasonable and appropriate insurance for a business of its nature and scale.

Part H
Legal

47. Term

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated in accordance with its terms.

48. Renewal

Engagements renew as set out in the applicable Commercial Schedule or Statement of Work.

49. Termination

Either Party may terminate on written notice if the other commits a material breach not remedied within 14 days, or becomes insolvent or subject to business rescue. On termination all licences cease and outstanding Fees fall due; Fees for Access Codes already issued are non-refundable. Clauses intended to survive do so.

50. Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for failure or delay caused by events beyond its reasonable control; the affected Party will mitigate and resume performance.

51. Dispute Resolution

Disputes are first escalated to senior executives; failing resolution within 15 Business Days, referred to mediation and then arbitration in Johannesburg, in English. Either Party may seek urgent interim relief from a court.

52. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa.

53. Notices

Notices are in writing to the notice details on the Signature Page; email notice is effective on delivery during Business Hours.

54. Assignment

Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other’s written consent, save that AfriversalAI may assign to an affiliate or in connection with a reorganisation.

55. Entire Agreement

This Agreement with its Supporting Publications is the entire agreement and supersedes prior discussions. Variations must be in writing and signed. If any provision is unenforceable, the remainder continues.

56. Electronic Signatures

This Agreement may be executed by electronic signature, which the Parties accept as legally binding and equivalent to a handwritten signature under the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002. Each signature is recorded with a date stamp, secure timestamp, verification ID, document fingerprint and verification barcode, and a signed PDF is issued to the Customer.

Order of Precedence

In the event of conflict, precedence is: (1) this Master Services Agreement; (2) the Statement of Work; (3) the Commercial Schedule; (4) the Purchase Order; (5) the Invoice.
