How It Works

Every module follows the same four phases.

The order is intentional — and different from most training. You engage with the case study before any theory is introduced. The learning comes from the tension between what you expected and what you find.

1

Encounter ~20 min

You receive the case study artifact — a real document, output, or scenario — and engage with it before any explanation. Task: "What do you notice? What would you do with this?" No theory yet. Your instincts are the starting material.

2

Reflect ~20 min

Guided questions surface what you were sensing. "What made you trust or distrust that output? Have you seen something like this at work?" You write before you're told the answer — because what you think matters for how you'll learn what comes next.

3

Concept ~30 min

The framework that explains what you just experienced is introduced. Concept content always connects back to the Phase 1 artifact — not generic theory pulled from a textbook. SA case studies throughout.

4

Apply ~30 min

A new scenario from your sector. Task: apply the Funda Five framework. Output: a brief written judgment — not just a feeling. This is where competency is built.
