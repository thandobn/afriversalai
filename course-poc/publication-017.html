Part A
Introduction

1. Purpose

This Policy establishes the principles governing the ownership, protection, licensing, development and authorised use of AfriversalAI’s intellectual property, brands, methodologies, learning content, software, research and innovation — safeguarding AfriversalAI’s proprietary assets while enabling customers and partners to benefit from them under clearly defined licence rights.

2. Scope

Applies to employees, directors, facilitators, contractors, customers, enterprise partners, referral partners, consultants and any person authorised to access or use AfriversalAI intellectual property.

3. Intellectual Property Principles

  • Innovation should be protected.
  • Knowledge should be responsibly shared.
  • Intellectual property is a strategic business asset.
  • Customers license AfriversalAI content — they do not acquire ownership.
  • Continuous innovation benefits all customers while preserving the integrity of the underlying intellectual property.
Part B
AfriversalAI Intellectual Property

4. Ownership

Unless expressly agreed otherwise in writing, all intellectual property created, owned, commissioned or developed by AfriversalAI remains the exclusive property of AfriversalAI, including present and future intellectual property.

5. Proprietary Frameworks

Funda Five™AfriversalAI™ThinkLab™Governance Forum™Strategy Lab™AfriversalAI Certified™

Together with the AI Literacy Framework, Enterprise Certification Framework, Member ID System, Programme Architecture, Assessment Methodology, Learning Journey Framework, Corporate Portal and Executive Dashboard. These are proprietary business assets.

6. Learning Content

AfriversalAI retains ownership of curricula, facilitator guides, learner guides, presentations, videos, graphics, assessments, case studies, templates, AI prompts developed by AfriversalAI, reports and implementation methodologies.

7. Software & Digital Assets

Including learning platforms, websites, mobile applications, Corporate Portal, reporting dashboards, APIs, databases, source code, automation workflows and AI assistants developed by AfriversalAI.

8. Research & Publications

Including white papers, research reports, industry frameworks, benchmarking studies, publications and learning methodologies.

Part C
Brands & Trade Marks

9. Brand Portfolio

The AfriversalAI brand portfolio may include registered or unregistered trade marks, trading names, logos, taglines, programme names, publication series and visual identities.

10. Trade Marks

AfriversalAI intends to protect its distinctive brands through appropriate trade-mark registration where commercially appropriate (e.g. AfriversalAI™, Funda Five™, ThinkLab™, Governance Forum™, Strategy Lab™, AfriversalAI Certified™). Use of these marks is subject to written authorisation where required.

11. Brand Usage

Customers and partners may use approved AfriversalAI branding only in accordance with written brand guidelines and any applicable licence. Brand assets must not be modified, distorted or used in a manner that could create confusion or imply unauthorised endorsement.

12. Customer References & Marketing

Neither Party shall publicly use the other’s name, logo or trade marks in promotional materials without prior written consent, except where required by law or expressly permitted. Approved marketing material supplied by AfriversalAI may be used by authorised partners only for promoting authorised services and must remain current and unaltered unless written approval is obtained.

Part D
Licensing

13. Customer Licence

Customers receive a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable licence to access and use AfriversalAI intellectual property solely for internal learning and workforce-development purposes during the term of the applicable agreement.

14. Partner Licence

Authorised partners may receive additional rights under a separate Partner Agreement. Such rights do not transfer ownership and remain subject to compliance with applicable agreements and brand standards.

15. Restrictions

Without prior written consent, no person may copy substantial portions of learning content; create competing programmes based on AfriversalAI materials; remove copyright notices; reverse-engineer software; distribute assessments; reproduce proprietary frameworks; or commercialise AfriversalAI intellectual property.

16. Derivative Works

Any derivative works, translations, adaptations or modifications require prior written approval unless expressly permitted under the applicable licence. Unless otherwise agreed, intellectual property in approved derivative works vests in, or is licensed to, AfriversalAI on agreed terms.

Part E
Innovation

17. Continuous Innovation

AfriversalAI will continue to enhance its methodologies, curriculum, technology and delivery models to reflect developments in AI, education, governance and industry practice. Customers benefit through their applicable licences.

18. Contributions & Feedback

Where customers, learners or partners contribute ideas, suggestions or feedback, AfriversalAI may consider and incorporate them. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, providing feedback does not transfer ownership or create an obligation to compensate the contributor.

19. Future Developments

Future enhancements, new frameworks, software modules, learning pathways or certifications developed by AfriversalAI remain its intellectual property unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Part F
Enforcement

20. Monitoring

AfriversalAI may monitor the use of its intellectual property and brand assets to identify unauthorised use, misuse or infringement.

21. Breaches & Reporting

Suspected unauthorised use should be reported promptly to ask@afriversal.ai. AfriversalAI may take appropriate contractual or legal action to protect its rights.

22. Legal Remedies

Nothing in this Policy limits AfriversalAI’s right to seek injunctive relief, damages or other remedies available under applicable law for infringement of its intellectual property rights.

23. Policy Review

This Policy is reviewed periodically to reflect changes in law, business strategy, intellectual-property registrations and the AfriversalAI product portfolio. Intellectual-property governance is overseen by an Intellectual Property Review Committee.
