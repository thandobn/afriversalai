Issued to Programme Administrators in Learning & Development, HR, Talent and Organisational Development. Every corporate customer must appoint a Programme Administrator — the day-to-day operational contact between the organisation and AfriversalAI.

Part A
The Role

1. Introduction

Congratulations on your appointment as the Programme Administrator for your organisation’s AfriversalAI learning programme. You are the primary operational contact between your organisation and AfriversalAI, central to ensuring the programme is implemented smoothly, learners remain engaged, and organisational objectives are achieved.

2. Your Role

The Programme Administrator coordinates programme activities on behalf of the Customer: coordinating learner enrolment; maintaining learner records; communicating programme information; supporting attendance; monitoring learner progress; coordinating with AfriversalAI; and assisting executive reporting.

3. Key Responsibilities

Before launch: confirm the learner list; verify employee details; coordinate internal communications; schedule launch activities; ensure participants understand expectations. During delivery: monitor participation; encourage engagement; resolve administrative queries; coordinate attendance; escalate issues where necessary. At completion: review completion reports; coordinate recognition; support executive reporting; identify future learning needs.

Part B
Before Programme Launch

4. Preparing for Launch

  • Learner list approved
  • Executive Sponsor confirmed
  • Internal communications distributed
  • Live session dates communicated
  • Programme calendar published
  • Project team informed

5. Learner Registration

Verify learner information; submit the approved learner register; confirm email addresses; review departmental allocations; notify AfriversalAI of required changes before launch.

6. Access Codes

Access Codes are issued after the commercial requirements in the applicable agreement have been satisfied. The Programme Administrator distributes Access Codes only to authorised learners and reports any suspected misuse.

7. Internal Communication

Suggested communications: executive announcement; programme launch invitation; live session reminders; assessment reminders; certification announcement; completion recognition. AfriversalAI can provide templates where agreed.

Part C
During Delivery

8. Monitoring Participation

Regularly review learner activation, module completion, attendance at live sessions, assessment completion and learners requiring additional support.

9. Attendance

Where live participation is required: encourage attendance; monitor absences; communicate changes promptly; coordinate make-up sessions where available.

10. Supporting Learners

The Programme Administrator is the first point of contact for routine administrative questions. Technical, assessment or certification matters are referred to AfriversalAI through the agreed support channels.

11. Reporting

Standard reports may include enrolment status, participation, completion, assessment outcomes and certification progress — supporting operational management and executive decision-making.

Part D
Programme Completion

12. Certification

Before certificates are issued, confirm that any required organisational information has been verified. Certificates are issued in accordance with Publication AAI-P016 (Certification, Assessment & Credentialing Policy).

13. Executive Reporting

At completion, AfriversalAI provides a summary report (participation, completion rates, assessment outcomes, certification statistics, observations and recommendations). Review it with the Executive Sponsor and stakeholders.

14. Programme Review & Future Pathways

A post-implementation review covers achievements, lessons learned, learner feedback, future learning opportunities and organisational priorities. Consider advanced learning pathways, leadership programmes, governance workshops, department-specific learning and follow-up capability assessments.
